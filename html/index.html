About Me

I'm a Computer Science undergraduate student at Rutgers University New Brunswick, where I'm also a software developer at the Laboratory of Computer Science Research. Here I take part in numerous clubs, find friends to go out and participate in hackathons, and play on the school CS:GO team.

When I'm not coding, you can find me at the park playing frisbee or riding my bike, at the arcade with friends playing rhythm games like Dance Dance Revolution, or at my school's LAN center practicing with the CS:GO team.

I am fueled by my passion for perfection in the field of computer science. I am eager to both build on my academic foundations and to stay in tune with the fast moving trends of the developer ecosystem through continued coursework, professional development, and independent projects.

A large part of my identity and a major driving force behind my passion is rooted in hacker culture. I enjoy taking a deep dive into programmable systems, exploring the minute details and stretching their capabilities. I love searching for hidden functionality, reverse engineering old games and programs, and overall doing novel things with systems that others might not have thought possible.
